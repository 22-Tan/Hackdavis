# VeriChain — Pitch Script

## 60-second stand pitch

> "Quick question — if a customer sent your support team a damaged-product photo
> tomorrow, could you tell whether it was real, or generated by Gemini in eleven
> seconds?
>
> Most companies can't. And it's not just e-commerce. Insurance claims, freelance
> portfolios, college essays, news photos, small businesses getting review-bombed
> — every system that runs on 'show me proof' is quietly breaking, and most of
> them don't have the vocabulary for it yet.
>
> The instinct is to build better AI detectors. We think that's a losing arms race
> — the adversary controls the content, every detector gets beaten six months later.
>
> So we flipped it. Instead of detecting what's fake, VeriChain proves what's real.
> We sign content cryptographically at the moment of capture. Any platform verifies
> it in milliseconds.
>
> Here's the demo. Green badge: signed at capture, real device, real time,
> auto-approved. Red badge: no signature, goes to human review. We didn't detect
> the fake. We just noticed the real one was missing.
>
> This is HTTPS for human content. The post-AI internet needs this layer."

---

## Key Q&A

**"Did you actually build this?"**
> "We built the UX demo and the concept design. The cryptographic primitives we'd
> use already exist in the C2PA libraries — rebuilding them in 24 hours produces
> a worse version of solved work. The contribution is the framing: provenance over
> detection, extending C2PA to the long tail."

**"Isn't this just C2PA?"**
> "C2PA is the right standard and we build on it. C2PA today is camera-OEM-side
> — Sony, Leica, Adobe. VeriChain extends it to the long tail: every browser,
> every phone, every web form. Same standard, much broader coverage."

**"What stops someone from screenshotting a real photo and re-signing it?"**
> "The signature is bound to the content hash. A screenshot has a different hash
> — the chain breaks instantly. On supported phones the key is also bound to the
> secure enclave, so a screenshot can't be signed as a fresh capture."

**"Isn't this still an arms race?"**
> "No — and that's the key insight. Detection is an arms race because the adversary
> controls the content. Provenance is a key-management problem. You can't forge a
> signature without the private key, no matter how good your generator gets.
> Cryptography doesn't get worse as AI gets better."

**"What about privacy?"**
> "We hash content before signing — the log never sees the actual photo or text.
> Verification proves that something with this hash was signed at this time on this
> device class. No content, no PII, no location unless the user opts in."
